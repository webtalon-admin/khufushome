### Investment Philosophy & Strategy

Future Super's investment approach is built on three pillars:

1. **Divestment (Negative Screening):** Exclude industries believed to be harmful, unsustainable, or a financial risk. The investment manager sets a **0% threshold** for direct exposure to fossil fuel companies.

2. **Reinvestment (Positive Screening):** Actively seek out companies and assets creating positive change in renewable energy, healthcare, education, technology, and sustainable infrastructure.

3. **Voting & Engagement:** Use shareholder voting rights and direct engagement to influence companies on climate, supply chains, and governance issues.

### Notable Exclusions (Negative Screens)

The fund screens out the following:

| Exclusion | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Fossil fuels** | Companies with fossil fuel reserves; mining, extraction, or burning of coal, oil, gas; fossil fuel power generators; significant services/financing to fossil fuel industry |
| **Weapons** | Weapons manufacturers |
| **Tobacco** | Tobacco companies |
| **Gambling** | Gambling companies |
| **Nuclear energy** | Uranium mining and nuclear energy |
| **Live animal exports** | Companies involved in live animal exports |
| **Major banks** | Australian Big 4 banks (CBA, Westpac, NAB, ANZ) and major international banks screened out due to fossil fuel financing |

### Notable Tilts / Positive Exposures

- **Technology bias:** Overweight tech sector (including AI beneficiaries like Nvidia) relative to benchmark due to fossil fuel exclusion freeing up allocation
- **Renewable energy:** Direct investments in solar farms, wind farms, battery storage
- **Social/affordable housing:** Investments in projects like Nightingale Housing
- **Carbon farming:** Investments via Conscious Investment Management (CIM)
- **Sustainable infrastructure:** Grid-scale batteries, logistics, circular economy companies

## Key Caveats

1. **Limited performance history:** The current Sustainable Growth option has only ~8 months of data (since 24 May 2025). Longer-term return comparisons can only be made using legacy predecessor options.
2. **Asset allocation detail:** Future Super does not publicly disclose a granular sub-asset class breakdown for the new options — only the 85/15 growth/defensive split.
3. **Returns basis:** All returns quoted are after investment fees, transaction costs, and taxes, but **before** the percentage-based administration fees and flat $60 admin fee.
4. **Fund restructure:** Data from the old "Future Super Fund" and new "Smart Future Trust" are not directly comparable due to different investment structures, even though the underlying investment philosophy remains the same.
5. **Fees above median:** Multiple comparison sources note that Future Super's fees are above the median for comparable risk-category options.
6. **Retail fund:** Future Super is classified as a retail fund (for-profit structure), not an industry fund.
