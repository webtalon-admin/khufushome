### Investment Philosophy

UniSuper follows a distinctive **internally managed, low-cost investment approach**:

1. **Significant internal management:** UniSuper has been progressively internalising asset management since 2009, managing a large proportion of assets in-house (particularly equities and property). This partnership model leverages external specialist expertise while maintaining internal control over portfolio decisions.

2. **Low-cost focus:** As a large industry fund, UniSuper passes scale savings through to members via competitive fees. The Growth option at 0.66% total is well below the industry average for actively managed diversified options.

3. **Active management with conviction positions:** The fund takes active positions in listed equities, with a relatively high allocation to listed Australian and international shares compared to some peers. The fund has historically had lower unlisted/illiquid asset exposure than some industry fund peers.

4. **Higher education heritage:** Originally serving the university and research sector exclusively, UniSuper became a public offer fund in July 2021. This heritage means strong governance and a highly engaged member base.

### Investment Philosophy

ART's investment approach centres on:

1. **Diversification through unlisted assets:** ART maintains a substantial allocation (~31.5%) to unlisted/alternative assets including real estate, infrastructure, private equity, and private credit. This is designed to deliver strong long-term returns while reducing share market volatility exposure.

2. **Long-term, disciplined strategy:** ART explicitly states they "don't design portfolios based on short-term economic, market, or geopolitical forecasts." The investment team seeks to capitalise on opportunities during periods of volatility.

3. **Active asset allocation:** ART employs an active asset allocation strategy that, as of September 2025, favoured bonds over shares and cash. Within equities, they preferred Japanese shares over US and Australian. In fixed income, overweight positions in France, UK, Italy, and Australia. Currency exposure underweight USD, favouring Asian and Latin American currencies.

4. **Scale advantage:** As Australia's 2nd largest super fund (~$300B+), ART has significant bargaining power for access to co-investments, large infrastructure deals, and lower external manager fees.

5. **Infrastructure as a key driver:** CIO Ian Patrick highlighted airport assets (rebounded to pre-COVID levels), data centre investments (including the successful divestment of AirTrunk), and private equity as key multi-year performance drivers.

## Key Caveats

1. **Different measurement dates:** UniSuper returns are as at 31 March 2025; ART returns are as at 30 September 2025. Direct comparison of compound returns requires caution.
2. **Year-by-year data limitations:** Neither fund publishes a full historical table of individual financial year crediting rates on their public websites. Individual FY returns have been sourced from media releases and third-party reports where available, and estimated relative to Chant West medians where not.
3. **UniSuper website access:** UniSuper's website employs bot-detection that blocks automated content retrieval, limiting the granularity of data obtainable for this report.
4. **Asset allocation basis:** UniSuper's actual allocation (from Morningstar/InvestSMART) may differ from the strategic allocation in the PDS. ART's figures are the strategic (target) allocation from 1 July 2025.
5. **"Unlisted assets" opacity:** ART's 31.5% "unlisted assets and alternatives" category bundles multiple sub-asset classes (property, infrastructure, PE, private credit). The internal breakdown is not publicly disclosed.
6. **Returns basis:** All returns are after investment fees, transaction costs, and investment taxes, but **before** administration fees and costs.
7. **ART option renaming:** ART's "High Growth" was called "Growth" until 1 July 2024. Pre-merger (pre-28 Feb 2022) data uses Sunsuper for Life Growth option returns.
8. **Chant West reclassification:** UniSuper Growth was reclassified from Chant West's High Growth category (81-95% growth assets) to Growth category (61-80%) in October 2023. This affects category-relative rankings.
