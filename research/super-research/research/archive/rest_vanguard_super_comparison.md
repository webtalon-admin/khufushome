### Key Observations

1. **Fee differential:** Vanguard is materially cheaper — approximately $128/year less on a $50,000 balance and ~$178/year less on $100,000. Over decades, this compounds significantly.

2. **Investment approach:** Rest uses active management with significant unlisted asset exposure (property, infrastructure, private equity). Vanguard is pure passive/index with 100% listed assets. This means Rest has more diversification across asset types but also less liquidity and valuation transparency.

3. **Track record:** Rest has a 37-year track record averaging ~8.3% p.a. since inception. Vanguard has only ~3 years of history, making meaningful comparison difficult. The short period has coincided with strong equity markets that favour passive strategies.

4. **Growth allocation:** Rest's Growth is slightly more aggressive (~77% growth assets) vs Vanguard's Growth (70% growth). For a more direct comparison, Vanguard's High Growth (90/10) or the Lifecycle option (age-dependent) may be more appropriate, though these have higher growth allocations than Rest's default.

5. **Unlisted assets:** Rest's ~20%+ allocation to unlisted assets (property, infrastructure, PE) provides diversification and may smooth returns. However, these assets have less frequent valuations and lower liquidity. Vanguard has zero unlisted exposure.

6. **Scale:** Rest is one of Australia's largest funds ($99B, 2M members), providing significant negotiating power and operational scale. Vanguard Super is small ($3.2B, 36K members) but backed by The Vanguard Group's global infrastructure and ~$14 trillion in global AUM.
