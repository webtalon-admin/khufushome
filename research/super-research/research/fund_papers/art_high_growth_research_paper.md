## 2. Investment Philosophy & Strategy

Australian Retirement Trust's investment approach is built on **diversification through unlisted assets**, **long-term disciplined strategy**, **active asset allocation**, and **scale advantage**. The fund holds one of the highest allocations to unlisted and alternative assets among mainstream super options—approximately 31.5% in property, infrastructure, private equity, and private credit combined. This diversification strategy aims to reduce correlation with listed equity markets and capture illiquidity premia over the long term.

Infrastructure has been a key driver of ART's performance. The fund's airport assets rebounded strongly post-COVID as travel demand recovered, and notable transactions such as the AirTrunk data centre divestment were executed at significant gains. The merger of Sunsuper and QSuper in February 2022 created a fund with exceptional scale—$300–370 billion in total assets—enabling access to exclusive deals, favourable fee negotiations, and in-house expertise across asset classes. The subsequent merger of Qantas Super in March 2025 further expanded the member base.

ART's philosophy emphasises patient, conviction-based investing with a focus on rolling return targets (CPI + 4.0%) rather than short-term benchmarks. The 85/15 growth/defensive split reflects a high-growth orientation suitable for members with long investment horizons. The fund's #1 ranking over 10 years by SuperRatings (10.11% p.a.) and three consecutive double-digit financial years (FY2023–FY2025) attest to the effectiveness of this approach, though the relatively new entity status post-merger means some historical data reflects predecessor funds.

### Commentary

- **Very high unlisted exposure (31.5%):** ART's allocation to unlisted assets—property, infrastructure, private equity, and private credit—is among the highest in the industry. This provides diversification, illiquidity premia, and reduced correlation with listed markets, but introduces valuation opacity and liquidity constraints. Assets are typically valued periodically rather than daily.
- **Minimal fixed income and cash:** With only 1% fixed interest and 2% cash, the fund is almost fully invested in growth-oriented assets. This maximises return potential but leaves little defensive buffer in sharp downturns.
- **Balanced listed equity split:** Australian and international shares are roughly equal (~32% each), providing geographic diversification across listed equity.
- **Infrastructure as key driver:** Airport assets, data centres (e.g., AirTrunk divestment), and other infrastructure have been significant contributors to returns.

## 7. Key Risks & Considerations

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **High unlisted exposure (31.5%)** | Unlisted assets are harder to value and less liquid than listed securities. In stress scenarios, redemptions may be constrained; valuations may lag market reality. |
| **Relatively new entity** | Formed Feb 2022 from Sunsuper + QSuper merger; Qantas Super merged Mar 2025. Integration risk and track record as a combined entity are still evolving. |
| **Option rename** | Renamed from "Growth" to "High Growth" (Jul 2024); ensure alignment with member expectations. |
| **Minimal defensive buffer** | 1% fixed interest and 2% cash leave little cushion in sharp equity downturns. |
| **Market risk** | 85% growth assets implies high volatility; negative years are possible. |
| **Concentration in tech** | Top international holdings (Apple, Nvidia, Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet) reflect significant US mega-cap tech exposure. |
| **Return target** | CPI + 4.0% over rolling periods; past performance does not guarantee future results. |
