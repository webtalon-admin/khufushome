## 2. Investment Philosophy & Strategy

AustralianSuper's investment approach rests on four pillars: **long-term investing**, **active management**, **global approach**, and **One Fund strategy**. The fund measures time horizons in years and decades to support wealth creation, avoiding short-term speculation. This long-term orientation aligns with superannuation's purpose and allows the fund to hold illiquid assets such as unlisted infrastructure and property.

Active management is central: AustralianSuper identifies assets, sectors, and markets it believes will outperform, with integrated ESG considerations. The fund invests globally to access the best opportunities and talent worldwide, rather than being constrained to domestic markets. The One Fund strategy leverages scale—$389 billion in assets and 3.5 million members—to negotiate favourable fees, access exclusive opportunities, and maintain in-house expertise across asset classes.

As a profit-for-member industry fund, all returns flow back to members rather than shareholders. The fund has consistently been rated among the lowest-fee large super funds in Australia, with a total MER of 0.69% that compares favourably to industry averages.

## 7. Key Risks & Considerations

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Market risk** | 70% growth assets implies moderate volatility; negative years can occur (e.g., FY2022 -2.97%). |
| **Concentration** | Top holdings (BHP, CSL, Westpac) represent meaningful single-name exposure. |
| **Unlisted asset valuation** | Unlisted infrastructure and property are valued periodically; liquidity may be limited in stress. |
| **Scale** | Large size can limit agility in some markets; however, scale also delivers fee advantages. |
| **Return target** | CPI + 3.8% over medium-long term; past performance does not guarantee future results. |
