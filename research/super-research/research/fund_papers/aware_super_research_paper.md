## 2. Investment Philosophy & Strategy

Aware Super's investment approach is built on a **diversified high-conviction strategy**, a **Whole-of-Fund model**, and a **~25–30% allocation to unlisted assets** managed by a dedicated private markets division overseeing approximately $47 billion. The fund targets CPI + 4.0% over rolling 10-year periods and employs a mix of passive and active managers across asset classes. Strong responsible investment integration is embedded throughout the investment process.

The Whole-of-Fund model means Aware Super coordinates asset allocation and manager selection across the entire portfolio rather than operating in silos. This enables better risk management, fee negotiation, and alignment of strategy with member outcomes. The private markets division—managing infrastructure, property, private equity, and credit—provides access to illiquid opportunities that can enhance diversification and long-term returns. The fund's formation from the merger of First State Super and VicSuper (July 2020), with WA Super joining in December 2020, created scale and capability. The opening of a London office in October 2023 signals ambition in global private markets.

Aware Super's High Growth option is the **most aggressive of mainstream funds** with an 88/12 growth/defensive split—higher than ART (85/15) and UniSuper (80/20). This makes it the default option for members aged 55 and under in the MySuper Lifecycle GROW stage, reflecting a long-horizon, growth-oriented profile. The fund has delivered three consecutive double-digit financial years (FY2023–FY2025), with 10-year returns of 9.27% p.a.

### Commentary

- **Zero fixed interest:** Aware Super's High Growth option holds no traditional fixed interest; defensive exposure is limited to 3% credit income and 4% cash. This maximises growth potential but increases volatility in bond-equity diversification benefits.
- **~25.5% unlisted (PE + Infra + Property):** Combined private equity (7%), infrastructure (11.5%), and property (7%) provide meaningful illiquid exposure. The private markets division manages ~$47B across these asset classes.
- **International equity tilt:** 38.5% international vs 28.5% Australian shares reflects a global orientation, with significant US mega-cap tech exposure in top holdings.
- **Most aggressive mainstream split:** 88/12 growth/defensive is the highest among comparable options, suitable for long-horizon members comfortable with volatility.
