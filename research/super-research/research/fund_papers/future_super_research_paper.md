## 2. Investment Philosophy & Strategy

Future Super's investment approach is built on three pillars: **divestment**, **reinvestment**, and **engagement**. The fund applies strict negative screening to exclude industries it considers harmful or financially risky—including fossil fuels, weapons, tobacco, gambling, nuclear energy, live animal exports, and the Australian Big 4 banks (due to fossil fuel financing). This ethical screen significantly reduces the investable universe but aligns the portfolio with members' values.

The fund actively reinvests in companies and assets creating positive change: renewable energy (solar, wind, battery storage), healthcare, education, technology, and sustainable infrastructure. A notable tilt toward technology—including AI beneficiaries like Nvidia—arises partly because fossil fuel exclusion frees allocation for growth sectors. Direct investments in solar farms, wind farms, and affordable housing (e.g., Nightingale Housing) complement listed equity exposure.

Future Super uses shareholder voting and direct engagement to influence companies on climate, supply chains, and governance. The fund is RIAA Certified and publishes a full holdings list for transparency. The 85/15 growth/defensive split reflects a medium-to-high risk profile suitable for members with a 12+ year investment horizon.

|---------|------------------|

**Fee comparison:** Future Super's fees are among the highest in the comparison set. On a $50k balance, members pay ~$700 vs ~$387 (AustralianSuper) or ~$668 (Hostplus Balanced). Over 30 years, the fee differential can materially reduce retirement savings. Members choosing Future Super are effectively paying a premium for ethical screening and impact investing.
