## 2. Investment Philosophy & Strategy

Hostplus's investment philosophy centres on **growth-biased diversification** with a **long-term horizon**. The fund targets CPI + 2.5–3.0% over rolling 20-year periods, reflecting a genuinely long-term mindset. Unlike many peers that target shorter horizons, Hostplus explicitly designs for multi-decade wealth creation.

A defining feature is **significant alternatives exposure**—approximately 32% of the portfolio—one of the highest among Australian super funds. This includes private equity (8.5%), venture capital, private credit, hedge funds, and other alternative strategies. The allocation aims to capture illiquidity premia and diversification benefits that traditional listed assets may not provide. Hostplus uses a **multi-manager approach** with JANA as external asset consultant, engaging specialist managers across asset classes including IFM Investors, Hosking Partners, Allan Gray, T. Rowe Price, and Baillie Gifford.

As a profit-for-member industry fund, all returns flow back to members. The higher fee level (1.23% MER) compared to AustralianSuper is largely driven by performance fees on alternatives—fees that are only charged when underlying managers outperform their benchmarks. FY2022 demonstrated strong downside protection: Hostplus Balanced returned -1.45% vs the median balanced fund of -3.44%, topping the SuperRatings league table.

## 7. Key Risks & Considerations

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Alternatives volatility** | Illiquid assets can be harder to value and sell in stress; performance fees can add to volatility of net returns. |
| **Below-median defensive** | ~6% in fixed interest and cash; less cushion in downturns than 70/30 peers. |
| **Higher fees** | 1.23% MER vs AustralianSuper 0.69%; fee drag over decades. |
| **Performance fee variability** | Performance fees can increase when alternatives outperform; reduces predictability of net fees. |
| **FY2022** | Despite beating median (-1.45% vs -3.44%), still negative; growth bias can amplify drawdowns. |
| **Long horizon** | 20-year target; shorter-term investors may experience more volatility. |
