## 2. Investment Philosophy & Strategy

Rest Super's investment approach centres on **active management** with a **quality long-term** orientation. The fund seeks to build diversified portfolios across public and private markets, with a particular focus on **real assets**—infrastructure, property, and alternative investments—that can provide stable cash flows and diversification benefits. Rest believes that patient capital and disciplined stock selection can add value over market-cap weighted indices.

The fund identifies and invests in **megatrends** that it expects to shape the global economy over the coming decades: deglobalisation and supply chain resilience, the clean energy transition, artificial intelligence and technology adoption, and demographic shifts including ageing populations. This thematic lens informs sector and geographic tilts within the portfolio. Rest's 77/23 growth/defensive split reflects a growth bias while maintaining meaningful defensive allocation for downside protection.

As a profit-for-member industry fund, all returns flow back to members. Rest's scale—nearly $99 billion and 2 million members—enables access to institutional investments and competitive fee structures. The fund's mid-range fees (0.66% + $78) sit between low-cost passive options and higher-fee alternatives-heavy peers.

## 4. Asset Allocation

| Asset Class | Target % |
|-------------|----------|

| International Shares | 35% |
| Property | 9% |
| Infrastructure | 11% |
| Alternatives | 2.5% |
| Debt / Fixed Interest | 14.5% |
| Cash | 4.5% |

**Growth / Defensive split:** 77% growth / 23% defensive

### Commentary

- **International equity tilt:** 35% international shares exceeds Australian allocation (23.5%); global diversification reduces home bias.
- **Real assets focus:** 20% combined in property (9%) and infrastructure (11%) provides exposure to unlisted assets and potential illiquidity premia.
- **Higher defensive allocation:** 14.5% debt and 4.5% cash (19% total) offers more downside cushion than many growth peers (e.g., 82/18 or 85/15 options); may dampen upside in strong equity markets.
- **Modest alternatives:** 2.5% alternatives is lower than alternatives-heavy peers such as Hostplus; more traditional asset mix.
