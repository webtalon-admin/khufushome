## 2. Investment Philosophy & Strategy

UniSuper's investment approach is distinguished by **significant internal management** since 2009, a **low-cost focus**, and **active management with conviction positions**. The fund manages a substantial portion of its portfolio in-house rather than outsourcing to external managers, which reduces fee leakage and allows for tighter alignment between strategy and execution. This internal capability has been a key driver of the fund's competitive fee structure—notably, UniSuper charges no percentage-based administration fee, only a flat $96 per year plus investment costs.

The fund's philosophy centres on long-term wealth creation through disciplined asset allocation and selective active bets. UniSuper maintains an 80/20 growth/defensive split, targeting CPI + 3.5% over rolling 7-year periods. The higher education heritage—UniSuper was originally established for the university sector—informs a culture of rigorous analysis and evidence-based decision-making. Since opening to public offer in July 2021, the fund has extended its low-cost, high-conviction approach to a broader member base.

UniSuper's active management style involves taking meaningful positions in assets where the fund has strong conviction, rather than broadly diversified passive exposure. This approach has contributed to strong historical returns, including the #1 ranked growth fund performance in calendar year 2024 (14.7%). The fund's lower unlisted exposure compared with peers such as Australian Retirement Trust and Aware Super reflects a preference for liquid, transparent assets and a more traditional listed-equity-centric portfolio.

### Commentary

- **Higher cash allocation (~12%):** UniSuper holds a materially higher cash allocation than many growth-oriented peers (e.g., ART High Growth ~2%, Aware High Growth ~4%). This may reflect tactical positioning, liquidity management, or a more conservative stance. In rising equity markets, elevated cash can act as a drag on returns; in downturns, it provides dry powder and downside protection.
- **Lower unlisted exposure:** Compared with ART (~31.5% unlisted) and Aware (~25–30% unlisted), UniSuper's ~5.3% property/infrastructure allocation is modest. This reduces valuation opacity and liquidity risk but may forgo some diversification and return potential from illiquid assets.
- **Strong listed equity tilt:** Combined Australian and international shares account for ~72% of the portfolio, giving the fund a clear equity-centric profile with high transparency and daily pricing.

## 7. Key Risks & Considerations

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **High cash allocation** | ~12% cash may be tactical or structural; in sustained bull markets, this can act as a drag on returns relative to peers with lower cash. |
| **Lower unlisted exposure** | May miss diversification and return benefits from infrastructure and property that some peers capture; conversely, reduces liquidity and valuation risk. |
| **Reclassification** | Chant West reclassified the option from "High Growth" to "Growth" (Oct 2023), indicating a more moderate risk profile than the name might suggest. |
| **Market risk** | 80% growth assets implies meaningful equity volatility; negative years are possible in market downturns. |
| **Concentration** | Top holdings (BHP, CSL, CBA, Westpac) represent meaningful single-name exposure. |
| **Return target** | CPI + 3.5% over 7 years; past performance does not guarantee future results. |
