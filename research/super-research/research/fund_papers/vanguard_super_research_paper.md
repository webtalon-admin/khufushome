## 2. Investment Philosophy & Strategy

Vanguard Super's investment approach is **pure index/passive**—the antithesis of active management. The fund tracks market-cap weighted indices across all asset classes, with **no active stock selection**, **no tactical tilts**, and **no unlisted assets**. This philosophy is grounded in Vanguard's 50+ years of global indexing experience and ~30 years operating in Australia. The core belief: over the long term, most active managers fail to beat their benchmarks after fees; low-cost indexing delivers market returns to members with minimal drag.

The Growth option holds **100% listed, liquid assets**—Australian shares, international shares (hedged and unhedged), Australian government bonds, and global fixed interest. There is zero exposure to private equity, unlisted infrastructure, unlisted property, or hedge funds. This transparency and simplicity appeal to members who prefer to know exactly what they own and who want the lowest possible cost structure.

Vanguard Super has grown rapidly since its October 2022 launch—membership doubled in FY2025—reflecting demand for low-fee, transparent super. The fund received the Money Magazine Best Value Super Product 2024 award. The 70/30 growth/defensive split is more conservative than many "growth" options (e.g., 77/23, 82/18), positioning the fund for members seeking moderate risk with minimal complexity.

**Growth / Defensive split:** 70% growth / 30% defensive

**Unlisted assets:** **0%** — 100% passive/indexed, 100% listed and liquid

### Commentary

- **Pure index approach:** All holdings mirror index compositions; no active management or security selection.
- **No unlisted diversification:** Zero exposure to private equity, infrastructure, or property; no illiquidity premia or alternatives-based downside protection.
- **Conservative growth split:** 70/30 is more defensive than many growth options (77/23, 82/18); suitable for risk-averse members or those prioritising cost over maximum growth.
- **Currency mix:** 29% unhedged + 13% hedged international equities provides natural currency diversification.

## 7. Key Risks & Considerations

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Limited track record** | Only ~3 years of data since Oct 2022 launch; no 5- or 10-year returns to assess long-term performance. |
| **No unlisted diversification** | Zero exposure to private equity, infrastructure, property; may underperform in regimes where alternatives provide downside protection or illiquidity premia. |
| **Conservative split** | 70/30 is more defensive than many growth options; may lag in strong equity bull markets. |
| **Small fund size** | $3.2B AUM vs industry giants ($100B+); less scale, though backed by Vanguard's $14T global platform. |
| **Passive approach** | Index tracking means the fund will never outperform the market; in periods when active managers add value, Vanguard will trail. |
| **No alternatives** | No hedge funds, private credit, or other alternatives for diversification or volatility dampening. |
