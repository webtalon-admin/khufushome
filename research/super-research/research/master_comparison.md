## Future Super: Key Concerns

1. **Highest fees in comparison:** 1.28% + $60/yr = $700/yr on $50k (vs $387 at AustralianSuper)
2. **Below-median historical returns:** Legacy Balanced Impact returned 5.94% p.a. since inception vs industry growth median of ~8-9% p.a.
3. **No track record on current option:** Restructured May 2025; Sustainable Growth has only ~8 months of data
4. **Small fund:** $2.2B AUM vs $300B+ at ART -- less scale, less bargaining power, less access to co-investments
5. **Retail fund (for-profit):** Unlike industry funds, Future Super has a for-profit structure
6. **Limited disclosure:** Does not publish granular asset allocation breakdown
7. **Ethical screens cost performance:** Excluding fossil fuels, banks, and resources removes significant portions of the ASX (BHP, CBA, Woodside, etc.)

### The Cost of Staying with Future Super

Assuming $100k balance, 9% market return, $15k annual contributions:
- **Future Super** (1.28% fees): ~$1.07M at age 60
- **AustralianSuper** (0.69% fees): ~$1.15M at age 60
- **Difference: ~$80,000+ lost to higher fees alone** (not including return differential)

If Future Super also underperforms by 1-2% p.a. on returns (as the legacy data suggests), the gap widens to **$150,000-$250,000+** over 25-30 years.
